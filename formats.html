×
  • 11-digit number
  • First 9 digits are random
  • To get the 10th digit: Multiply the first 9 digits by numbers from 10 down to 2, add them up, divide the total by 11. If the remainder is less than 2, the result is 0. Otherwise, subtract the remainder from 11.
  • To get the 11th digit: Take the same first 9 digits plus the 10th digit, multiply them by 11 down to 2, repeat the same math.

  • Ex. CPF: 12345678909
    First 9 digits = 123456789

    Step 1: Calculate the 10th Digit
    Multiply each digit by weights from 10 to 2:
    1 × 10 = 10
    2 × 9 = 18
    3 × 8 = 24
    4 × 7 = 28
    5 × 6 = 30
    6 × 5 = 30
    7 × 4 = 28
    8 × 3 = 24
    9 × 2 = 18
    Total = 210

    Now calculate the remainder of the total when divided by 11:
    210 / 11 = remainder of 1
    Then apply the CPF rule:
    -If remainder < 2 → 10th digit = 0
    -Else → 10th digit = 11 − remainder
    So: 11 − 1 = 10 → 10th digit = 0
    1234567890

    Step 2:Calculate the 11th Digit
    Now use the 10 digits, and multiply by weights from 11 to 2: 1 × 11 = 11
    2 × 10 = 20
    3 × 9 = 27
    4 × 8 = 32
    5 × 7 = 35
    6 × 6 = 36
    7 × 5 = 35
    8 × 4 = 32
    9 × 3 = 27
    0 × 2 = 0
    Total = 255
    Now divide by 11 again and take the remainder:
    255 / 11 = remainder of 2
    Apply the rule again:
    -If remainder < 2 → 11th digit = 0
    -Else → 11th digit = 11 − remainder
    So: So: 11 − 2 = 9 → 10th digit = 9

    Final CPF = 12345678909

  • 14-digit number
  • First 12 digits are random (digits 1–8: company root; 9–12: branch code, often 0001)
  • To get the 13th digit: multiply those 12 digits by weights 5 4 3 2 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2, sum, divide by 11. If remainder < 2 → digit = 0; otherwise → digit = 11 − remainder.
  • To get the 14th digit: take the same 12 digits plus the 13th, multiply by weights 6 5 4 3 2 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2, sum, divide by 11, then same “<2→0, else 11−remainder” rule.
